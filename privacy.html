Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 22, 2025

Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains how Soul Care Counselling (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects your personal information in connection with our website and services. We aim to align with Canadian privacy requirements, including PIPEDA and applicable provincial standards for health information where relevant.

1) Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us and information collected automatically:

  • Contact details (e.g., name, email, phone) when you inquire, book, or join an interest list.
  • Client intake & clinical information relevant to assessment and therapy (subject to health privacy laws and professional standards).
  • Billing information as required to process payments (handled by our third-party payment/booking provider).
  • Technical data such as IP address, device/browser type, and pages visited, collected via cookies or similar technologies for basic site functionality and performance.

2) How We Use Your Information

  • To provide counselling, care coordination, and client support.
  • To schedule, confirm, and manage appointments.
  • To process payments and issue receipts.
  • To respond to inquiries and send essential service messages.
  • To maintain security, troubleshoot issues, and improve our website and services.
  • To meet legal, regulatory, and professional obligations.

We rely on your consent and/or other lawful bases (e.g., contract, legitimate interests, legal requirements) depending on the activity and jurisdiction.

3) Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information:

  • Service providers who support booking, telehealth, payments, email, or IT/security—only as needed to perform their services and subject to appropriate safeguards.
  • Legal/professional obligations, such as duty to report, court orders, or to protect safety and prevent harm.
  • With your consent, for coordination of care or other requested purposes.

4) Cookies & Analytics

Our site may use essential cookies for functionality (e.g., navigation, page rendering) and limited analytics to understand site performance. You can adjust your browser settings to manage cookies. Disabling some cookies may affect site features.

5) Telehealth & Booking Platforms

We use reputable third-party platforms for booking, telehealth, and payments. When you follow links to these platforms, your information will be processed according to their privacy policies and security practices. Please review those policies before submitting information on their sites.

Book via our secure portal

6) Information Security

We take reasonable physical, technical, and administrative measures to safeguard personal information against loss, theft, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we encourage you to use strong, unique passwords and keep them confidential.

7) Retention

We keep personal information only as long as necessary to provide services, meet legal and professional standards (including health record retention rules), resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. When no longer required, we aim to securely delete or de-identify information.

8) Your Rights

Subject to applicable laws, you may have rights to:

  • Access your personal information and request a copy.
  • Request corrections to incomplete or inaccurate information.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent (does not affect prior processing).
  • Complain to a privacy regulator if you believe your rights have been infringed.

To exercise your rights, contact us using the details below. We may need to verify your identity.

9) Children’s Privacy

We provide services to children and youth with appropriate consent/authorization in line with applicable laws. If you are a parent/guardian with questions about a child’s information, please contact us.

10) International Transfers

Some service providers may store or process information outside of your province or outside Canada. In such cases, information may be subject to the laws of those jurisdictions. We aim to work with providers that implement appropriate safeguards.

11) Changes to this Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will revise the “Last updated” date and, where appropriate, notify you by reasonable means.

12) Contact Us
